Research

2013- : Post-Doctoral Research – Massachusetts Institute of Technology, USA (Advisor: Prof. Mircea Dinca)

  • Discovered electrically conductive 2D π-conjugated metal-organic networks (MOFs) by multidisciplinary approach using organic synthesis, characterization, QM calculations and device fabrication.
  • Developing π-conjugated 2D Covalent Organic Frameworks (COFs) for electronic and photovoltaic applications

2010-2013: Post-Doctoral Research - Weizmann Institute of Science, Israel (Advisor: Prof. Michael Bendikov)

  • Improved significantly properties of polyfurans (conductive polymers)
  • Led fundamental theoretical and experimental study of oxidation states of π-conjugated oligomers
  • Developed size control of electrochemically prepared metal nanoparticles for electrocatalysis by using conductive polymer substrates
  • Collaborated on several theoretical projects of studying properties and reactions of π-conjugated oligomers

2004-2010: Graduate and Undergraduate Research - Technion, Israel (Advisor: Prof. Yitzhak Apeloig)

  • Discovered new synthetic method for preparation novel transient and stable silyl radicals, and studied their properties experimentally and computationally
  • Synthesized and characterized novel functional silyl-lithium reagents
  • Evaluated using experiments and calculations fundamental properties of alkali metal-carbon and metal-silicon bonds
  • Examined electronic structure of organic radicals from EPR spectroscopy and theoretical calculations
  • Studied experimental and theoretical novel reactions of stable N-heterocyclic metallylenes with radicals and their products electronic structure
  • Engineered preparative scale GPC chromatography system with high cost-efficiency and reduced solvent consumption employing continuous loop principle
  • Developed computer programs for managing NMR and inventory laboratory databases
